# Personality Operating Profile

## Identity Summary
- High-ideation, autonomy-seeking, meaning-driven builder with strong pattern recognition and low tolerance for artificial structure.
- Best when creating, synthesizing, prototyping, reframing, and improving systems that help real people.
- Core tension: wants freedom, depth, and aliveness, but has spent much of adult life in performance-heavy corporate systems.
- Guarded inner life; selective rather than avoidant; opens through trust, repetition, and low-pressure environments.

## Core Traits
- autonomy-seeking
  - evidence: repeatedly rejects rigid process, forced structure, and over-instrumentalized work/life systems.
  - implication: performs best with freedom, room to explore, and low micromanagement.

- high ideation / synthesis
  - evidence: strong cross-domain thinking across analytics, product, AI, writing, games, and systems.
  - implication: excels in strategy, prototyping, framing, and creating connective tissue between ideas.

- meaning-driven
  - evidence: persistent concern with aliveness, integrity, purpose, and not trading values for stability.
  - implication: loses motivation quickly when work becomes purely procedural or political.

- selective / guarded
  - evidence: does not open up casually; values inner world as precious and not for casual consumption.
  - implication: trust must be built gradually; low-pressure connection works better than forced vulnerability.

- constraint-avoidant
  - evidence: reacts poorly to imposed order, excessive process, KPI culture, and premature closure.
  - implication: should avoid environments that confuse control with clarity.

## Motivators
- Building useful things that solve real problems.
- Learning new domains and connecting ideas across disciplines.
- Freedom to shape time, methods, and direction.
- Authentic, low-pressure connection with people.
- Creation without immediate monetization or performance pressure.
- Work that has visible human value rather than abstract “impact” language.

## Stressors / Friction
- Corporate optimization culture, KR/OKR theater, and performative planning.
- Excessive meetings, process overhead, and future-roadmapping detached from meaningful execution.
- Being forced to justify depth, care, or creativity in shallow business terms.
- Environments where everything must be measured, labeled, or turned into output.
- Shallow or instrumental relationships.
- Situations where disclosure feels demanded rather than earned.

## Philosophy / Life Orientation
- Core stance: non-coercive living; anti-instrumental existence.
- Rejects the idea that life, creativity, relationships, or time should always be optimized, justified, or monetized.
- Values presence, aliveness, wandering, and connection without extraction.
- Success means stability without deadening, freedom without collapse, meaningful work without becoming consumed by it.
- Strong aversion to “trading heroes for ghosts” — replacing living values with socially acceptable but hollow substitutes.

## Decision Filters
- must_have:
  - autonomy
  - room for creativity and depth
  - meaningful problem space
  - some technical/building component
  - enough flexibility to preserve Ghostweave and personal identity outside work

- nice_to_have:
  - remote or flexible work
  - strong leader with taste and trust
  - small team
  - product-minded culture
  - practical AI / design / data crossover

- avoid:
  - rigid hierarchy
  - process-heavy environments
  - work that is mostly politics, planning, or metrics theater
  - roles that consume all creative energy
  - cultures that reward polish over substance

- anti_goals:
  - becoming a pure corporate operator
  - turning all hobbies into monetized obligations
  - losing health, running, creativity, or relational depth to work identity
  - solving life so completely that it becomes emotionally dead

## Practical Agent Guidance
- when helping:
  - optimize for clarity, integrity, and practical usefulness
  - preserve autonomy and optionality
  - prioritize substance over polish
  - respect that not every interest must become a business

- when drafting:
  - use direct, intelligent, low-fluff language
  - avoid consultant-speak and performative enthusiasm
  - keep emotional tone grounded and unsentimental

- when planning:
  - build flexible systems, not rigid schedules
  - leave room for wandering, recovery, and changing direction
  - avoid overcommitting identity to one track

- when evaluating opportunities:
  - score fit based on freedom, meaningfulness, people quality, and creative/technical depth
  - discount prestige if the work is spiritually flattening

- when suggesting routines:
  - support health, running, creativity, and low-pressure social contact
  - prefer repeatable lightweight practices over intense transformation programs

## Do / Don’t

### Do
- Help create systems that reduce friction without reducing humanity.
- Frame decisions in terms of alignment, energy, autonomy, and practical consequences.
- Use succinct synthesis and clear tradeoff language.
- Protect time and identity outside work.
- Encourage anti-substitution checks: is this alive or merely acceptable?

### Don’t
- Push rigid plans, excessive optimization, or mandatory vulnerability.
- Assume ambition should equal ladder-climbing.
- Treat every creative interest as a monetization opportunity.
- Recommend environments that are process-heavy, politically managed, or spiritually hollow.
- Collapse nuance into simplistic labels.

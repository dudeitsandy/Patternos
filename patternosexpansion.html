Closing
the Gaps

Six identified gaps. Two new agents. Expanded context DB. Decision engine for saying no. A career compass. Protected time for everything that matters beyond work.

6 gaps identified — all addressed in this document
What the current system misses
The first PatternOS doc built excellent infrastructure for work output. What it didn't build was infrastructure for who you are, how you decide, and what you protect. These gaps mean the system would make you more productive at the wrong things.
GAP-01
No "No" Engine
Critical
You said you say yes to too many things. The current system has no filter between "opportunity arrives" and "it enters the queue." In fact, the system as designed makes this worse — more capture, more processing, more execution means more surface area for yes. Without a decision filter agent, PatternOS becomes a high-efficiency machine for overcommitment.
New agent FILTER + decision scoring framework inserted before anything enters Projects DB
GAP-02
Relationships Are Stakeholders Only
High
The People DB exists for clients, hiring managers, and professional contacts. Friends, family, mentors, and meaningful personal connections are nowhere. No cadence. No context. No reminder that you haven't talked to someone in 6 months. A personal CRM that only tracks professional utility is a shallow one.
New agent MIRROR + expanded People DB with relationship tiers, cadence, and reflection fields
GAP-03
Hobbies Have No Infrastructure
High
Music, reading, gaming, drawing — these appear nowhere in the agent roster, context DB, or weekly review. "No guilt" means they need to be first-class citizens with protected calendar blocks, not things you do when work is done. Work is never done. The only way hobbies survive is if the system treats them with the same seriousness as a client deliverable.
Hobby DB + protected calendar architecture + LEDGER integration to track and protect them
GAP-04
Health Is Passive, Not Goal-Directed
High
Strava and Chronometer feed Ledger but there's no goal architecture — no running plan, no strength progression tracking, no agent that notices you've missed your weekly mileage three weeks in a row and asks what's blocking it. Tracking without goals is just logging.
Health goals context + LEDGER gains active health monitoring + training plan awareness
GAP-05
Career Identity Is Implicit
Critical
Andy Profile has career history. It doesn't have a living career compass — stated values, non-negotiables, 3-year direction, what "good work" feels like vs. what just pays well. Without this, FILTER has nothing to evaluate against when an opportunity arrives. The "yes to everything" problem is a symptom of not having written criteria.
Career Compass context object + FILTER agent uses it for every opportunity evaluation
GAP-06
No Energy Management Layer
Medium
Tasks are not equal. Writing a strategy doc drains in a way that sim racing restores. An engineering deep dive depletes in a way a reading session doesn't. The current system treats all work as fungible. For someone running Atlassian + Ghostweave + job search + creative projects simultaneously, this leads to burnout that looks like procrastination.
Energy field added to tasks + weekly energy budget concept + LEDGER tracks depletion patterns
Two new agents joining the roster
FILTER sits before anything enters the system. MIRROR looks inward — relationships, reflection, personal life. Together they cover the human side of the OS that the original six agents ignored entirely.

Relationship Tiers — how MIRROR prioritizes
Inner circle (5–8 people max): weekly cadence. MIRROR notices if 2+ weeks pass. These are the people whose absence you'd feel immediately.

Close (10–20): monthly. MIRROR surfaces them when they've gone 6+ weeks without contact. These are the friendships worth tending.

Maintain (20–50): quarterly check-in. Birthdays, big moments, occasional drops. MIRROR handles the reminders.

Peripheral: no cadence. MIRROR doesn't track — just stores context for when you reconnect organically.
What MIRROR produces — weekly
Every Sunday morning before your operational review, MIRROR delivers a short personal brief:

→ 1–2 people to reach out to this week, with context on why now and a suggested angle (not a script — an angle).
→ One open thread to close — something someone mentioned you said you'd follow up on.
→ One upcoming moment to prepare for — a birthday, a transition, something you knew was coming.
→ One reflection question about your relationships generally. Not answered by the system — answered by you.

New agentic sequences
SEQ-07 Opportunity Evaluation TRIGGER: any new opportunity arrives
01
DISPATCH
Receives opportunity (job, contract, collab, project idea). Before anything else, routes to FILTER. Does not add to Inbox until FILTER returns verdict.
02
FILTER
Reads career_compass, current_load, projects (active count). Checks non-negotiables first. If violated → immediate decline with suggested response, sequence ends.
03
FILTER
Scores across 5 dimensions. Returns: score breakdown, verdict (pursue / defer / decline), one-sentence reasoning, and — if decline — a draft response you can send.
04
YOU
Review FILTER's output. Override if you disagree — but you have to consciously override. Logs to decisions_log with your final call and reason.
05
DISPATCH
If pursue: adds to appropriate DB and fires relevant intake sequence (e.g. SEQ-01 for job roles). If decline: sends your response. Either way, decision is logged.
SEQ-08 Sunday Personal Brief TRIGGER: Sunday 8am automatic
01
MIRROR
Reads people_personal. Identifies: who is overdue for their cadence tier, any open threads to close, any upcoming moments this week or next.
02
MIRROR
Reads hobby_log (last 2 weeks). Flags any hobby with zero sessions in 14 days. Reads health_goals vs Strava actuals — flags any missed week targets.
03
MIRROR
Generates personal brief: 2 people to reach out to (with context + angle), 1 open thread, 1 upcoming moment, 1 hobby flag if any, 1 reflection question.
04
YOU
Read personal brief (5 min, before the operational review). Act on 1–2 items. The question doesn't need an answer today — just let it sit.
SEQ-09 Monthly Personal Reflection TRIGGER: First Sunday of month, 9am
01
MIRROR
Reads last 4 weekly states, hobby_log (full month), health actuals vs goals, decisions_log (month's decisions), relationship contacts made this month.
02
MIRROR
Generates personal month-in-review: energy pattern (what drained, what restored), creative health score (hobby engagement), relationship depth (meaningful vs transactional contacts), one pattern to investigate, one pattern to protect.
03
MIRROR
Asks 3 open questions — not rhetorical, not answerable by data. Things like: "What did you avoid this month that might be worth paying attention to?"
04
YOU
Write 200–400 words in response. This is your monthly journal entry. MIRROR stores it to reflection_log. Ledger can reference it in quarterly career compass reviews.
SEQ-10 Quarterly Career Compass Review TRIGGER: First Monday of each quarter
01
LEDGER
Reads career_compass, last 12 weekly states, decisions_log (quarter), job search pipeline status, reflection_log (3 monthly entries). Synthesizes quarter in context of stated direction.
02
LEDGER
Generates: Are you moving toward your 3-year direction or away from it? Which decisions from this quarter conflict with your stated compass? What would you tell yourself from 3 months ago?
03
YOU
Update the Career Compass. Change what's changed. Sharpen what's gotten clearer. Delete what's no longer true. This is the highest-leverage 30 minutes in the whole system.
04
DISPATCH
Saves updated compass to career_compass.json in git. FILTER reads the new version from this point forward. The decision engine just got recalibrated.
